---
title: "Variational Inference for Heteroscedastic Semiparametric Regression"
collection: publications
venue: 'Australian and New Zealand Journal of Statistics'
---

We develop fast mean field variational methodology for Bayesian heteroscedastic semiparametric regression, in which both the mean and variance are smooth, but otherwise
arbitrary, functions of the predictors. Our resulting algorithms are purely algebraic, devoid
of numerical integration and Monte Carlo sampling. The locality property of mean field
variational Bayes implies that the methodology also applies to larger models possessing
variance function components. Simulation studies indicate good to excellent accuracy, and
considerable time savings compared with Markov chain Monte Carlo. We also provide
some illustrations from applications.
